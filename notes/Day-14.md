# Day 14

## What did I build?

Today I practiced SQL aggregate functions using my inventory table. I wrote queries to count records, calculate totals, find averages, and determine minimum and maximum values. I also combined aggregate functions with WHERE clauses to answer more specific business questions.

## What did I learn?

I learned how aggregate functions summarize data instead of returning individual rows. I used COUNT(), SUM(), AVG(), MIN(), and MAX() to analyze inventory data, and I learned that aggregate queries can be combined with WHERE to produce filtered summaries. I also learned how to use AS to give calculated columns meaningful names.

## Biggest challenge today

The biggest challenge was shifting from retrieving rows to thinking about summarizing data. I needed to decide which aggregate function matched each business question.

## How did I solve it?

I solved this by working through several examples and exercises, identifying whether each question required counting, totaling, averaging, or finding extreme values. Repetition helped reinforce when each function should be used.

## What surprised me?

I was surprised by how much useful business information can be obtained from just a few aggregate functions. Simple queries can answer questions that would otherwise require manual calculations.

## One thing I still don't understand

I'd like to learn how aggregate functions work with larger databases and how to group results by categories, such as finding totals or averages for different types of products. I expect grouping will make these functions even more powerful.
